The **Vow** is the manifesto's closing pledge and its emotional core: a person should be able to do many things. Not many versions of one thing, but many things across wildly different registers — technical, commercial, manual, domestic. Ship the feature and close the deal. Fix the network and butcher the argument. Model the building, write the manifesto, change the diaper.

What the Vow asks for is competence held as range, and held without apology. It is not a boast about being good at everything; it is a refusal to accept that a single craft must consume a whole life. To take the Vow is to become [[The Generalist]] on purpose, by choice rather than by failure to commit.

This is where Heinlein's old cry lands — [[Specialization Is for Insects]] — and where [[The Generalist Manifesto]] stops arguing and simply promises.
