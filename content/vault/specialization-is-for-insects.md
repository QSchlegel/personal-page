**Specialization Is for Insects** is the manifesto's rallying cry, and it is borrowed. The line belongs to Lazarus Long in Robert A. Heinlein's *Time Enough for Love* (1973), where it caps a litany of human competences — change a diaper, plan an invasion, write a sonnet, set a bone, comfort the dying, die gallantly — before landing the verdict. The American "z" survives on purpose: it is a direct quotation, not house spelling.

Read it as a dare, not a slur. The motto is provocative by design — it wants an argument — but it is not contempt for depth. The manifesto closes, after all, in praise of [[The Specialist]]; the insult is aimed not at mastery but at the shapeless middle, the unchosen competence that is neither wide nor deep.

What the line demands is intention: be broad on purpose or deep on purpose, but refuse the default — the discipline [[The Vow]] asks of anyone who takes [[The Generalist Manifesto]] seriously.
